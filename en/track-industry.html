Topics of Interest

Topics of interest are listed below. List is not exhaustive.:

  • Requirements survey, collection, analysis, documentation, management, traceability, validation, verification, and commitment
  • Requirements engineering education and training
  • Requirements engineering for specific software development paradigms, e.g., industry-oriented, agent-oriented, scenario-oriented, model-drive, or service-oriented
  • Requirements engineering for specific domains, e.g., mobile appliactions, web applications, or mission-critical applciations
  • Requirements engineering in agile methodologies
  • Requirements specification languages, methods, and tools
  • Model-oriented software engineering
  • Compliance with legal and regulatory frameworks

Submission Guidelines

The Industry Track aims to foster cooperation between academia, industry, and the services sectors through posters, brief application reports, papers, and discussions. This track seeks to bring together professionals from these sectors to share their knowledge and experiences with the Requirements Engineering community. The separate industry track will host papers that specifically report on problems and experiences related to Requirements Engineering and tools in practice. These papers will be reviewed by a separate committee, including representatives from the industry, and assessed based on their practical relevance rather than their scientific merit.

The Industry Track allows for showcasing the latests initiatives, exchanging experiences, and networking among Requirements Engineering experts. Organizations are invited to submit their cases and present them to an international audience. The Industry Track will have:

  • Case presentation papers, which will receive submissions and with the incentive of the Requirements Engineering Innovation Award for the most outstanding case; and
  • Invited speakers to present and discuss future trends in Requirements Engineering.

The case presentation papers can be at most eight pages long and must follow LNCS page format. All papers must be submitted through the Easychair submission system in PDF format only. Submissions must be written in Portuguese, Spanish, or English. The paper title must be written in Portuguese, Spanish, and English.

Any paper that exceeds the page limit, lies beyond the scope of the conference, or does not follow the formatting guidelines will be desk-rejected.

Case presentation papers must contain the following elements:

  • Abstract
  • Introduction
  • Case Description
  • Solution and Results
  • Conclusion and Lessons Learned
  • Bibiographic References: at least 3 bibliographic references

All submissions will be peer-reviewed by at least three members of the Program Committee.

All accepted papers will be published with Open Access through the WERpapers repository.
