Specifying Intelligence: RE for the Next Generation of AI Systems

Conferencista: Marcos Kalinowski

Título: Specifying Intelligence: RE for the Next Generation of AI Systems

Institución: Pontifícia Universidade Católica do Rio de Janeiro

Resumen: As artificial intelligence (AI)–enabled systems increasingly integrate diverse paradigms (e.g., machine learning, reinforcement learning, symbolic AI, foundation models, and multi-agent systems), the role of Requirements Engineering (RE) becomes more important than ever. Building trustworthy and adaptable AI systems begins not with algorithms, but with requirements that clearly define what the systems should do, under which conditions, and within what constraints. While RE has advanced in supporting machine learning, our understanding of requirements for systems that combine multiple AI paradigms remains limited. Each paradigm introduces unique assumptions and behaviors that challenge traditional RE methods. This keynote argues that combining AI paradigms is akin to composing high-level components of adaptable intelligence. RE professionals must now combine core RE expertise with AI literacy and abstraction skills to effectively engage in the engineering of intelligent behavior. Capturing paradigm-specific assumptions and trade-offs early in the development process is difficult but essential to align system capabilities with stakeholder goals. These challenges are also a call to action: the RE community faces an opportunity to develop empirically grounded, paradigm-aware methods to support the specification of complex, evolving, and trustworthy AI systems. By placing RE at the center of AI development, we can shift from opportunistic design to intentional engineering of intelligent behavior.

Résumé: Marcos Kalinowski is a Professor of Software Engineering at PUC-Rio, Brazil. His research focuses on AI Engineering, Empirical Software Engineering, and Human Aspects of Software Engineering. Before transitioning to academia, he accumulated over a decade of industry experience. He co-leads the ExACTa PUC-Rio lab, where he supervises PhD research and leads R&D projects with industry partners to develop AI engineering solutions and real-world AI systems. His lab's work has resulted in award-winning AI deployments and multiple US patents. He has authored several books and over 200 research publications, earning 20 distinguished paper awards, including ACM Distinguished Paper Awards at ICSE and CAIN. He holds a research productivity distinction from the Brazilian Research Council (CNPq) and a 'Scientist of the State' distinction from Rio de Janeiro's state research agency (FAPERJ). He serves the community as associate editor of the Journal of Systems and Software, editorial board member of the Empirical Software Engineering journal, and as part of the organizing and program committees of several international conferences. He is an active member of ACM, IEEE, ISERN, and the Brazilian Computer Society.
